Another year in the books. 2025 was a year of transition — some things ended, new things began, and I learned more than I expected along the way.

## What went well

**Writing consistently.** I published more this year than any previous year. Not all of it was great, but the volume helped me find my voice. The best pieces came from topics I was genuinely wrestling with, not topics I thought would perform well.

**Saying no more often.** I turned down projects that didn't excite me. This was hard at first — it felt like leaving opportunities on the table. But the space it created let me go deeper on the things that mattered.

**Learning new tools.** I picked up a few new technologies this year that changed how I work. The initial investment felt slow, but the compounding returns have been worth it.

## What I'd do differently

I spent too long on a couple of projects that I should have abandoned earlier. The sunk cost fallacy is real, and I'm still learning to recognize when persistence becomes stubbornness.

I also didn't take enough breaks. Sustainable pace is something I understand intellectually but struggle with in practice.

## Looking ahead

For 2026, I want to focus on depth over breadth. Fewer projects, more investment in each one. More time reading and thinking, less time reacting. Let's see how it goes.
