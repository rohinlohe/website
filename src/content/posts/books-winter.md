Winter is my favorite season for reading. The shorter days and cold weather make it easy to justify long stretches with a book and a cup of tea.

## The highlights

**"Seeing Like a State" by James C. Scott** — A fascinating look at how large institutions try to make the world legible, and how those efforts often backfire. Changed how I think about top-down planning.

**"Several Short Sentences About Writing" by Verlyn Klinkenborg** — Not a writing manual so much as a way of seeing. Every sentence in this book earns its place. I've been re-reading sections before I sit down to write.

**"The Mushroom at the End of the World" by Anna Lowenhaupt Tsing** — About matsutake mushrooms, global supply chains, and what it means to live in the ruins of capitalism. Weird and wonderful.

## What I noticed

This winter I gravitated toward books that challenge how I see systems — political, ecological, linguistic. I think there's a thread connecting all of these: they're all about the gap between how things are supposed to work and how they actually work.

## Up next

I have a growing stack that includes some fiction I've been putting off. Spring feels like the right time for novels.
